PlayStation 5 Price And Release Date Revealed

Erik Kain Senior Contributor Opinions expressed by Forbes Contributors are their own. Games Share to Facebook Share to Twitter Share to Linkedin

The PS5 will be cheaper than many expected.

Credit: Sony

During today’s PlayStation 5 Showcase, Sony unveiled the console’s price and release date, and it’s a very competitive offering when compared to the Xbox Series X and Series S.

The PS5 will cost $499 while the All Digital edition of the console is just $399 despite having the exact same specs (other than no UHD Blu-Ray drive).

This means that you can buy a PS5 for $100 cheaper than the Xbox Series X. It’s almost like deja vu all over again.

The Xbox Series S still clocks in at the lowest price-point at just $299, but it’s a less powerful system than the others by a pretty wide margin.

The PS5 will release just two days after its rival next-gen system, hitting shelves on November 12th. There’s still no word on pre-orders, but it may be a very difficult console to find at launch due to high demand and reported production bottlenecks due to the ongoing global pandemic.

The consoles are all launching just days after the US presidential election, amidst what will almost certainly be one of the most contentious elections in US history. It will be an interesting time, make no mistake. Perhaps not an ideal time to launch a new console, though many of us will be happy for the distraction.

The rest of the showcase was fine. We saw some gameplay from games like Call Of Duty: Black Ops Cold War, Deathloop, Resident Evil Village and the Demon’s Souls remake. But both Sony and Microsoft have a long ways to go to convince consumers that these are essential purchases right now.
