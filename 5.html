Sony announces PlayStation 5 release date and price - as it happened

Tonight Sony announced a price and November release date for the new video games console, along with new games

Updated The Sony PlayStation 5 and its controller - a date and price for the new console will be announced tonight Photograph: Sony Interactive Entertainment I/Reuters

9.54pm BST 21:54

Sony announces release date, price and new games for PlayStation 5

Here’s everything that just happened (you can scroll down to see all the trailers and footage):

-The PlayStation 5 is out November 12th in the US, Japan, Canada, Mexico, Australia, New Zealand and South Korea

-For the rest of us in the UK and Europe it will be out on November 19th

-The price is £449/$499/499 euros (digital-only edition at £359/$399/399 euros )

-Square-Enix announced Final Fantasy XVI as a PlayStation Exclusive

-Hogwarts Legacy, a Harry Potter universe game from Warner Bros, was announced for 2021

-A new God of War game was briefly teased

-Anyone who buys a PS5 and subscribes to PlayStation Plus will have access to a library of PS4 classics at launch through PlayStation Plus Collection

And that’s it. Once again, scroll down to watch all the trailers from the games shown tonight.

8.42pm BST 20:42

Of course, since June, Microsoft has announced plenty more info about its rival console(s), the Xbox Series S and Xbox Series X. The whole of Twitter has been arguing about whether or not the marketing is confusing - which, to me, is a fairly good indication that it IS confusing - but here’s the basic info:

-The Xbox Series S is an entry-level version of the new Xbox that will cost £249. It has no disc drive and cannot play games in 4K.

-The Xbox Series X will cost £449 and outputs in 4K, and has a disc drive

It’s a super aggressive price point from Microsoft - and coupled with Xbox Game Pass, which offers access to every new game from Microsoft’s studios, every new EA game and hundreds more for just over £10 a month, it’s really turned some heads. Xbox is even offering interest-free payment plans that can get you into the next generation of consoles for £20.99 a month. That’s very attractive, and cheaper in real terms than a cutting-edge games console has ever been before.
